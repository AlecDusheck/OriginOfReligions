The Silk Road

Beyond the Maurya Empire, the most powerful spreading method of Buddhism was The Silk Road.

Many traders on the Silk Road were indeed Buddhist (Liu 58). Due to the fact that the Silk Road connected both India and China, it served as the perfect channel on influence. As the number of Buddhist missionaries increased, Buddhism in China increased drastically.

The influx of Buddhism into China also led to the building of Buddhist monasteries being built.
